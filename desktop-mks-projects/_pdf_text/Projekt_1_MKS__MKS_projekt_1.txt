1 Problem statement
We want to analyze a simplified model of two competing populations with
a coupled logistic system. We want to analyze this models fixed point, its
periodic orbits, dynamics and long-term behaviour.
2 Model and parameters
2.1 Problem 1; Fixed points
We model two populations, with normalized densities
xn andy n
We are given the model for the very next generation, it is a combination of
the two logistic updates:

yn+1 = (1−ε)r 2yn(1−y n) +εr 1xn(1−x n).(2)

4.1 Problem 1; Fixed points

4.3 Problem 3; Phase Portraits
How to interpret a phase portrait.A phase portrait plots the pairs
(xn, yn) after the transient. If all plotted points collapse onto one point, the
orbit approaches a stable fixed point. If the points alternate between a small
number of isolated locations, then the system approaches a periodic orbit. If
the points spread over a larger set, then the dynamics is more irregular and
may be chaotic.
Figure 1 corresponds to the parameter set (r 1, r2, ε) = (2.8,2.9,0.1). In
this case, the orbit collapses onto a single point in the phase plane. This
indicates that the dynamics approaches a stable fixed point. The coupling
is relatively weak and the growth parameters are moderate, so the long-time
behaviour is simple and stable.
Figure 2 shows the case (r1, r2, ε) = (3.1,3.4,0.3). Here the phase portrait
consists of two distinct points. This is characteristic of a period-2 orbit: after
the transient, the system alternates between two states instead of converging
to a single fixed point.

6 Discussion
The three examples illustrate how the qualitative dynamics changes as the
parameters increase. For smaller values ofr 1 andr 2, the system tends to
settle to a fixed point. For intermediate values, the system may converge to
a periodic orbit. For larger values, the dynamics becomes much more com-
plicated and the phase portrait spreads over a wider set. The code therefore
does more than just produce pictures. It gives a direct numerical way to
distinguish between fixed-point behaviour, periodic behaviour, and more ir-
regular dynamics by looking at the geometric structure of the long-time orbit
in the phase plane.
